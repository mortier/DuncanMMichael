A  Bit About Me:

     Firstly, I am a person who loves to create. I find the beauty in the world through gears, cams, and linkages, in high energy systems on the verge of conflagration, and in using really-really flowery language to describe what I like to work with. 
     Hi, I'm Duncan. It's my third year at Olin and in the past few years I've done some pretty cool stuff: made projects I would have thought impossible in high school, learned more than I ever had previously, and have had about three hours of sleep...total...in three years...I may have done my math wrong by a few orders...eh, what's a few orders of magnitude among friends?
     I was born and raised in San Francisco by my art-director mother and chef father. I love to cook, and occasionally try to do photography (with mixed success). When I was fifteen I went off to boarding school on the Big Island of Hawai'i where I learned to scuba dive, cliff dive, surf, pound taro, eat spam, ride horses, spear fish, play rugby, pole vault, be a singing WWII Austrian captain with seven children, and probably some school stuff too...eh who remembers? 
    Now, at Olin, I pretty much never have 'work' as I'm way to busy figuring out this really cool dynamics problem, or figuring out why Solidworks decided that the part that was fine yesterday is suddenly broken. What I'm saying is that work here rarely feels like work because it's all stuff I would want to do anyway.
