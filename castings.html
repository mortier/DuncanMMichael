Casting of metals has, in some form or another, been possible for thousands of years. It is only since the invention of 3D printers and lost plastic casting, has it become a practical process for those of us that lack the skills necessary to carve impossible shapes out of wax. It is also a viable alternative to the much more expensive and much less tried metal 3D printers. Due to its relative ease and cost-effectiveness, casting has become a key part of a number of my projects and will continue to be so in future.
To learn the casting process, as well as to learn about micro structure of alloys I designed a series of rings of which the final iteration is pictured.
When my blind sailing team received our 43 year old O'Day 20 it came with a severely damaged tabernacle and mast-step setup (left). We replaced said step with a larger, stronger, lost-plastic casted aluminum step of our own design (right). Centered is our backup 3D printed mold for if the casting didn't work the first (second) time.
Moscow Mules are cocktails that are traditionally served in copper mugs because of copper's high heat conductivity witch allows the liquid to be cooled rapidly without a superfluous amount of ice which would dilute the flavor. Both myself and a friend are rather partial to these cocktails. We also are partial to working with dangerous things like liquid metal. So the decision to make our own with lost plastic casting, was an obvious one.
